Security & privacy

JawnRemote can move your mouse, type, and power your PC — so it's built to stay on your own network and nowhere else.

LAN-only by design
The server's firewall rule is scoped to your local subnet (remoteip=localsubnet), so the port is invisible to the internet — not just sitting behind your router.
Refuses outside connections
Even if that port were forwarded or exposed, the server drops any client whose address isn't on your private network.
PIN pairing
No mouse move or keystroke is accepted until a client sends the PIN shown in the PC server window.
Brute-force lockout
Too many wrong PINs from one address triggers a temporary lockout, so it can't be guessed by hammering.
No cloud, no account
Your phone talks straight to your PC over Wi-Fi. Nothing is relayed through a third-party server, and there's no sign-in.
No ads, no tracking
No advertising SDK, no analytics, no Advertising ID — there's nothing to collect.

Quick start

Get going in 2 minutes

Step 1 — Install the PC server

  1. Download JawnRemote-Server-Setup.exe (button above) and run it.
  2. Click Yes on the one Windows prompt, then Next → Install → Finish.
  3. A small window opens showing your PC's address and a PIN. Leave it running.
The installer opens the firewall for your local network automatically (on any Wi-Fi profile) — the thing that breaks other remotes on Windows 11.

Step 2 — Get the app (or just use a browser)

  1. Install the JawnRemote app on your phone (the Download for Android button above) for the full feature set.
  2. No app? On any device on the same Wi-Fi, open the browser address shown in the PC server window (e.g. http://192.168.x.x:8770/) and enter the PIN — trackpad, clicks, scroll and typing, nothing to install.
  3. Make sure the device is on the same Wi-Fi as the PC.

Step 3 — Connect

  1. Open the app. Your PC appears under Discovered — tap it.
  2. Enter the PIN from the server window. That's it.
  3. (If it isn't found automatically, tap Add PC and type the IP shown on the PC.)

Troubleshooting

App can't find or reach the PC

Using a VPN?

This is the most common cause. A VPN on your phone (or PC) pulls all network traffic into its encrypted tunnel — including the local connection to your PC — so the app's packets get routed off to the VPN server instead of across the room, and it times out.

Fix it in your VPN app's settings — either of these works:

  • Turn on Local network sharing — sometimes labeled "Allow local network access," "LAN access," or "Allow access to local devices." Local traffic then skips the tunnel while everything else stays protected.
  • Or use split tunneling to exclude JawnRemote from the VPN.
Most VPNs have one of these (Mullvad, NordVPN, Proton, ExpressVPN…). After changing it, reconnect in the app.

On the same network?

The phone and PC must be on the same Wi-Fi. Watch out for Guest networks and "client isolation" / "AP isolation" router settings — these block device-to-device traffic even on the same Wi-Fi. Put both on your normal network.

Still nothing?

  • Tap Add PC and type the IP shown in the server window — auto-discovery is the first thing a VPN or strict router blocks, but a direct connection to the IP still works.
  • Make sure the server window is still open on the PC.
  • The installer opens the firewall for you, but third-party security software can re-block it — allow JawnRemoteServer.exe on private networks if prompted.
