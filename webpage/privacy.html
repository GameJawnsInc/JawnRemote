JawnRemote — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 31 May 2026 · Jawnston Inc.

JawnRemote ("the app") lets you use your phone as a mouse and keyboard for your own Windows PC over your local Wi-Fi network. This policy explains what data is and isn't involved.

What the app does with your data

  • No account, no cloud. The app connects directly to a server you run on your own PC, on your own local network. We do not operate servers that receive your activity, and we do not require sign-in.
  • Stored only on your device. The PCs you save (name, IP address, port, and PIN) and your settings are stored locally on your phone. They are not sent to us or to anyone else.
  • What you type and where you point goes only to the PC you're connected to. It is never sent to us.

Advertising

The free version shows ads via Google AdMob. To serve and measure ads, Google's advertising SDK may collect your device's Advertising ID and related device/usage information, in line with Google's advertising policies and privacy policy. We do not receive or store this information ourselves.

You can purchase the one-time Remove Ads option in the app to stop ads, and you can reset or limit your Advertising ID at any time in Android Settings → Privacy → Ads.

Purchases

The optional "Remove Ads" purchase is processed by Google Play Billing. We receive only confirmation that the purchase was made; we never see your payment details.

Permissions

The app uses the Internet/network permission to reach your PC, and includes the advertising-ID permission used by the ads SDK above. It does not request access to your location, contacts, photos, microphone, or camera.

Children

JawnRemote is a general-purpose utility and is not directed at children under 13.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update the date above and post the new version at this URL.
